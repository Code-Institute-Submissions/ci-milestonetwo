France

The French national rugby is coached by Fabien Galthié and they play their home matches at Stade de France in Paris. They currently rank at Number 5 in the World according to the IRB Rugby World Rankings. They have never won a World Cup but have been beaten three times in the final. Behind England they would be the second best Six Nations performing team in the World Cup. As well as being Runner-Up three times, they have placed Third once and Fourth twice. They have played in Nine all World Cups to date.

Stade de France is now considered their home ground. Previously they played their matches at Parc des Princes also in Paris but they moved from there at the end of 1997. Stade de France has a capacity of 80,000. The construction commenced on 2 May 1995. The stadium was inaugurated on 28 January 1998. The national rugby team's first match in the facility was contested on 2 February 1998, with France earning a 24–17 win over England in front of 77,567 spectators.

The 2007 Rugby World Cup was help in France so the Stade de France along with many other stadiums in France were used throughout the tournament. The opening match of the tournament was played here between France and Argentina. With Argentina beating the hosts by 5 points. There were two more groups matches played here along with one Quarter Final, both Semi-Finals and the Final. France were beaten in the Semi-Final by England who lost to South Africa in the Final.
